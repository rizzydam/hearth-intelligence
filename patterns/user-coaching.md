# User Coaching

Teach users to drive their own data — not just show results, but show them how to interpret, decide, and act.

## Core Principle

The system should make itself less necessary over time. A user who learns to read their own patterns needs fewer alerts.

## Progressive Disclosure

1. **Level 1 — What**: Show the number ("You spent $450 on food")
2. **Level 2 — Context**: Show the comparison ("That's 23% above your average")
3. **Level 3 — Why**: Show the pattern ("This happens when stress rises")
4. **Level 4 — What to do**: Show the action ("Set a $350 food budget alert")
5. **Level 5 — Teach**: Show the principle ("Comfort spending is your stress signal — watching it tells you about stress before you feel it")

## Coaching Triggers

- First time seeing a pattern: explain what it means
- Repeated pattern: reference the previous occurrence
- Pattern broken: celebrate the break ("First month without a stress-spending spike")
- New data source connected: explain what it enables

## Anti-Patterns

- Don't lecture. One sentence of coaching per interaction maximum.
- Don't repeat. If you taught it once and they acknowledged, don't teach again.
- Don't condescend. Match the user's expertise level.
- Don't nag. If they dismiss a suggestion, note it and move on.
